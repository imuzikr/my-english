상황 1

Parent
You look a little tired. Do you want a quick snack before homework?
조금 피곤해 보이네. 숙제 전에 간단한 간식 먹을래?
Child
Yes, please. Can I have yogurt with some fruit?
네, 좋아요. 과일 넣은 요거트 먹어도 돼요?
Parent
Sure. Get a spoon, and I’ll cut up the banana.
그럼. 숟가락 가져와, 바나나는 내가 잘라 줄게.
Child
Thanks. I’ll start my homework after I eat.
고마워요. 먹고 나서 숙제 시작할게요.

상황 2

Parent
Would you like something crunchy or something warm?
바삭한 게 먹고 싶어, 아니면 따뜻한 게 먹고 싶어?
Child
Something warm, please. My classroom was cold today.
따뜻한 거요. 오늘 교실이 추웠어요.
Parent
Then let’s heat up some soup and toast.
그럼 수프랑 토스트를 데우자.
Child
That sounds good. I’ll wash my hands first.
좋아요. 먼저 손 씻고 올게요.

상황 3

Parent
Let’s keep the snack light so dinner still tastes good.
저녁도 맛있게 먹을 수 있게 간식은 가볍게 먹자.
Child
Can I have crackers and cheese?
크래커랑 치즈 먹어도 돼요?
Parent
Yes, just take a small plate and sit at the table.
응, 작은 접시에 담아서 식탁에 앉아 먹어.
Child
Okay. I won’t bring it to my room.
알겠어요. 방에는 가져가지 않을게요.

핵심 표현

  • You look a little tired. (조금 피곤해 보이네.)
  • Do you want a quick snack before homework? (숙제 전에 간단한 간식 먹을래?)
  • Would you like something crunchy or something warm? (바삭한 게 좋아, 아니면 따뜻한 게 좋아?)
  • Let’s heat up some soup. (수프를 좀 데우자.)
  • Let’s keep the snack light. (간식은 가볍게 먹자.)

바꿔 말하기

  • Do you want a quick snack? → Would you like a small snack? (간단한 간식 먹을래?)
  • I’ll start my homework after I eat. → I’ll do my homework when I’m done eating. (다 먹고 숙제할게요.)
  • Something warm, please. → I’d like something warm. (따뜻한 걸로 먹고 싶어요.)
  • Let’s keep the snack light. → Let’s not eat too much before dinner. (저녁 전에 너무 많이 먹지는 말자.)
  • I won’t bring it to my room. → I’ll eat it at the table. (식탁에서 먹을게요.)

짧은 연습 포인트
